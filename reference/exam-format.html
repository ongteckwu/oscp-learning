OSCP+ Exam Format

The rules of the game. Everything below is verified against OffSec's official help portal — re-read the live exam guide close to exam day, as OffSec can change details.

As of 1 November 2024 the certification is OSCP+. Same course (PEN-200), same exam — but the format changed in ways that older guides get wrong.

OSCP vs OSCP+

Both are earned from the same PEN-200 course and the same exam.1 The "+" is the current designation; it adds enhanced Active Directory content and removed bonus points.

AspectOSCP (base)OSCP+ (current)
Earned fromPEN-200 examSame PEN-200 exam
ValidityLifetime, never expires"+" expires after 3 years
If "+" lapses—You keep the lifetime OSCP, minus the "+"

Maintaining the "+": keep active maintenance and either pass a recert exam within 6 months of expiry, pass another qualifying OffSec exam, or complete OffSec's CPE program.1

Active Directory set403 chained hosts (10 / 10 / 20); partial AD points now possible
The arithmetic that matters The 40-point AD set is the largest single block — you cannot reach 70 comfortably by ignoring it. A common passing shape: full AD (40) + one full standalone (20) + one foothold (10) = 70. Plan to be competent at AD, not just standalones.

What is NOT on the current exam

Don't waste time here
  • Stack buffer overflow — removed in the PEN-200 2023 update.3 It is no longer in the exam body of knowledge. (Many older study plans still drill it — skip.)
  • Bonus points — removed at the Nov 2024 OSCP+ transition.1 The old format gave 10 bonus points for course exercises + lab boxes. Do not plan around them.
  • AI / LLM tools — strictly prohibited during the exam.2 (Yes, including me. Learn it for real — that's the whole point.)

The report

A PDF, archived as a .7z and uploaded via OffSec's portal (200 MB limit). Every script and proof-of-concept must be included as text inside the PDF. Results come within ~10 business days.2 The report is graded — a working exploit you can't document clearly does not earn points. We will treat report-writing as a first-class skill, not an afterthought.
